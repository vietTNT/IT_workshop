IDOMS

“crack the books” = start studying
“The reason why he isn’t doing very well is that he rarely cracks the books.”

“cut class” = miss school
“Although some of my daughter’s friends cut class a lot, she is at school every day.”

VOCABULARY

  • stuff (noun): things
    ”Please put your stuff in your backpacks.”
  • bow (verb): bend your head and waist to show respect and thanks to another person
    ”You should bow in Japan when you meet people in formal situations.”
  • math (noun): mathematics
    ”I don't like math. It's hard for me.”
  • sweetheart (noun): a person that you have a romantic relationship
    “She eventually married her childhood sweetheart.”
  • That's my boy: you are really my son “Good job! That's my boy.”
  • auntie (noun): an aunt
    “My uncle and auntie are coming to visit us.”
  • have a crush on someone: to have a strong but temporary feeling of liking someone romantically
    “I thought I had this huge crush on her for about two years.”
  • warm-heart (adjective): kind, helpful
    “She helped me do housework when I got injured. She's really a warm-hearted person.”
  • belongs with: when you and someone have the same thought or some features, that means you belong with him/her
    “Logan is extremely into Indie music. I thought he belongs with me because I love Indie music too.”
